lo.fish is a new programming language created by W., currently still work in progress. lo.fish is designed to capture the essence of the Yoneda Lemma in Category Theory, which can be understood as follow: "Everything we need to know about an object in a category is completely determined by its arrows to every object in that category."

------------

In a way, the Yoneda Lemma is the category-theoretic correspondence of anattā in Buddhism.

------------

According to Shakyamuni's Dharma, anattā (Pali: 𑀅𑀦𑀢𑁆𑀢𑀸; directly translated as *none-self* or 無我) is one of the three marks of existence. Anattā is synonymous with Anātman (an + ātman) in Sanskrit. Anattā can be understood as a strategy to attain non-attachment to Samsara by recognizing that no phenomenon (inside Samsara) can have a permanent, unchanging "self" or essence.

Understanding and realizing the true nature of anattā in daily meditation and mindfulness practices is at the core of cultivating samatha-vipassanā i.e. "to gain tranquillity-insight into the true nature of reality". 


> "These two qualities have a share in clear knowing. Which two? Tranquillity (samatha) & insight (vipassanā).
>
>  
>
> "When tranquillity is developed, what purpose does it serve? The mind is developed. And when the mind is developed, what purpose does it serve? Passion is abandoned.
>
>  
>
> "When insight is developed, what purpose does it serve? Discernment is developed. And when discernment is developed, what purpose does it serve? Ignorance is abandoned.
>
>  
>
> "Defiled by passion, the mind is not released. Defiled by ignorance, discernment does not develop. Thus from the fading of passion is there awareness-release. From the fading of ignorance is there discernment-release."
>
>  
>
> --- AN 2.30 (Vijja-bhagiya Sutta: A Share in Clear Knowing)
>
> --- translation by Thanissaro Bhikkhu
